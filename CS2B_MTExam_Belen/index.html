Tourist Spots & Adventures

Aw-Asen Falls

This waterfall remains hidden in a valley in Sigay, Ilocos Sur. It is so majestic that visitors cannot fail to marvel at its stunning beauty.

#aw-asenfalls #iloscossursigay #falls #natureadventures #hike

Skyline & Sakura in Quirino Ilocos Sur

Skyline also called 'Sea of Clouds'famous in one Ilocos Sur and one of the best destination. Mostly visited at 5:00 am by riders. Going down a bit to skyline you will discover Quirino's Sakura Blossoms.

#quirinoilocossur #skylineviewdeck #sakuraquirino #seaofcloudilocos #natureadventures

Candon City Eco Park

Peaceful nature park in Candon City, Ilocos Sur, with verdant foliage, picnic grounds, and a serene atmosphere for families and travelers. It is a community site for recreation, leisure strolls, and activities, enhancing eco-tourism.

#candoncityilocossur #candonecopark #palacapacecoaprk #picnic #natureadventures

Local Products

Robusta Coffee ₱50

Locally grown coffee variety known for its strong flavor, high caffeine content, and with bitter taste.

Tilapia ₱200 per kilo

Raised in small community ponds, tilapia provides a sustainable source of fresh fish for families and local markets.

Balitaw Rice ₱150 per kilo

Traditional heirloom rice variety with a distinct dark purple to black color, chewy texture, and nutty flavor.

Bamboo Bags ₱200-350

Handcrafted eco-friendly bags made from woven bamboo strips, showcasing the community's creativity and sustainable use of local materials.

Angaki Coffee ₱50

A coffee blend from Quirino, usually made from robusta beans, with a bold aroma and rich taste

Dried Mangoes ₱50-80

Sweet, chewy, and flavorful mango slices that are sun-dried or dehydrated, making them a popular pasalubong.

Coconut HandCrafts ₱100-250

Handmade souvenirs crafted from polished coconut shells, turned into bowls, ladles, and decorative items.

Eco Park Milk ₱25-50

A new, locally made dairy item of Candon Eco Park, with a creamy flavor and natural taste. It promotes sustainable agriculture and rural livelihoods and provides tourists a healthy beverage

Kalamay ₱40-50

A sweet sticky rice cake prepared with glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and sugar. Sold in banana leaves or plastic wrap. Extremely popular pasalubong item.

Tobbacco ₱150-500

Candon is the premier tobacco-producing region. Leaves are cured and treated ("flue-curing") for cigarette and other tobacco products.

Chichacorn / Kornik ₱30-100

Crunchy corn snack—deep-fried or popped corn bits, flavor-ed. Favorite among locals and tourists.

Puto/Sinambung ₱60-200

A Candon City's traditional delicacy composed of glutinous rice, coconut milk, and sugar, typically wrapped in banana leaves and steamed. It is sweet, soft, and sticky in texture, thus a popular local snack and pasalubong.
